# employee-attrition-analysis

## Business Case
Nowadays, employee attrition poses a significant challenge to organizations, both in terms of cost and operational stability. Employee resignation not only affects the to direct expenses such as searching and training the new employee to replace the previous role, but also causes indirect impacts like decreased team productivity. As a result, this project aims to identify the main factors that contribute to the employee attrition using the real Human Resource dataset that was found in Kaggle. By understanding these factors, it can help to provide a valuable insights for the employers and managers to develop proactive strategies to retain their workforce.

## Data Science Questions
1. What is the frequency of the overall employee attrition based on gender?
2. How does employement status differ across their department and job role?
3. How do monthly income influence employees' work-life balance and their opinions to stay or leave the organization?
4. Do employees with low job satisfaction and high overtime show a higher tendency to leave?
5. Can we predict whether an employee will leave the company based on their age, environment satisfaction and years at company?
6. Do employees with family life tend to stay longer in the company?
7. What is the relationship between employee's last promotion with their performance rating and how does this relationship affect their perception of staying or leaving the company?
8. Which combination of JobLevel and MonthlyIncome can be adjusted to reduce employee from leaving?
